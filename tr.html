Frequently Asked Questions

What is a tournament rating (TR)?

It's a rating that shows how well you've performed in Kentucky tournaments. It functions much like ELO. Everyone starts with a rating of 1500.0. After each tournament, all the matches are recorded and a new TR is determined for each participant. Each match, the difference in TR is calculated and the winner is rewarded TR while the loser loses TR. TR is zero sum, so the amount gained/lost per match is the same for both people. If the favorite of a match wins, TR changes less. And if the underdog wins, the TR will change more.

Are all tournaments used for TR?

No. A tournament must be in person and have at least 8 active participants (8 players, but someone DQd doesn't count) in order to be used for TR.

Do out of state competitors get a TR?

They do, but they are not given a rank on the leaderboard. They will instead be labeled as an "Invader". I try my best to identify who is out of state and who isn't, but I only have so much information to work with. If you notice someone is labeled as an invader but shouldn't be or vice versa, please let me know either by Twitter or E-Mail.

Are there resets or seasons?

The current plan is to start a new leaderboard at the beginning of every year. That being said, it won't be a complete reset. It will be more of a rating squishing. TRs will be halved by their distance from 1500.0. So a player with a TR of 1700.0 would be pushed back to 1600.0. And a player with a TR of 1400.0 would be pushed to 1450.0. Additionally, players won't appear on the new year's leaderboard unless they play in a eligible tournament in the new year. Finally, since I can't possibly keep track of an accurate TR for out of state competitors, they're all reset to 1500.0.

Why isn't there a TR Leaderboard for [insert game]?

If it's a popular game, I'm probably working on it. I want to have a TR for every game that is played in Kentucky. I'm currently working backwards with newer games first as they have less tournament history to cover.
